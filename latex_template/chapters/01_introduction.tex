\section{Introduction}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

The 2024 United States presidential election was one of the most intensely followed political events in recent history. Public attention spanned every corner of the internet while financial markets shifted in response to campaign developments. The stakes were high enough that people began betting on the outcome through prediction markets such as Polymarket, where daily odds on a Trump victory fluctuated continuously throughout the campaign. This raises an intriguing question: can the digital footprint left by public attention and sentiment actually predict where those odds are headed?
This project investigates exactly that, using data from six sources, Bluesky, Reddit, MediaCloud newspapers, Google Trends, financial markets, and Wikipedia-sourced polling averages, to combine descriptive analyses with predictive modelling aimed at forecasting daily Polymarket Trump win probabilities. Together, these sources offer a multidimensional view of public attention that may encode electoral momentum before it is fully reflected in market prices.
This project offers value to anyone seeking to understand how digital public discourse relates to political forecasting, from campaign strategists to journalists.

The full codebase and data pipeline for this project are available at: \url{https://github.com/Annaerme/group-project-SMWA}.
