\section{Descriptive Modeling}

\subsection{EDA}

Polymarket odds display persistent momentum rather than mean-reversion (Figure \ref{fig:polymarket_chart}), motivating the inclusion of autoregressive features in the predictive model. Election buzz across all three sources accelerates toward election day and spikes transiently around key campaign moments (Figure \ref{fig:electionbuzz}), with attention to both candidates moving largely independently, justifying their treatment as separate signals rather than a zero-sum pair. Financial indicators move in internally consistent clusters (Figure \ref{fig:financials_sp500_corr}), suggesting a shared underlying risk factor and motivating the use of derived features such as returns and rolling volatility over raw price levels. Google search interest reinforces this picture (Figure \ref{fig:gt_polymarket}): Trump-related queries are more tightly coupled to prediction market odds than Harris-related ones, supporting search behaviour as a genuine informational signal rather than mere campaign salience.

Most strikingly, polling averages remained essentially flat throughout the campaign while Polymarket odds drifted directionally toward Trump (Figure \ref{fig:polls_eda}), a divergence that is itself informative and motivates the explicit inclusion of a poll--market divergence feature in the predictive model.

\subsection{Network analysis}

Three network analyses were conducted, one per media source. For Bluesky, a directed weighted mention graph was constructed from mentions between accounts. In-degree centrality (share of all mentions received) and PageRank (endorsement by influential accounts) were combined in a matrix to map mention reach (top panel of Figure \ref{fig:centrality_landscape}). For newspapers, an undirected source-source graph was built using cosine similarity between each outlet's TF-IDF-weighted title vocabulary, with edges retained above the 85th percentile of non-zero similarities. The resulting network is visualised in the top panel of Figure \ref{fig:news_source_network}, with a zoom-in on the top 40 sources in the bottom panel. The top 20 outlets by degree, betweenness, and PageRank, grouped and colour-coded by media leaning, are shown in Figure \ref{fig:news_centrality_top20}. For Reddit, a subreddit-overlap graph based on Jaccard similarity of author sets was complemented by a TF-IDF keyword-similarity network between subreddits (Figure \ref{fig:keyword_similarity}) and a centrality analysis on the user engagement graph, combining in-degree centrality with PageRank to map which posts keep receiving comments from influential commenters (bottom panel of Figure \ref{fig:centrality_landscape}). An echo-chamber analysis was additionally conducted on both Reddit and Bluesky using the buzz-cluster tags inherited from preprocessing. On Reddit, cross-cluster overlap was measured separately for post authors and for commenters: for each pair of buzz clusters, the set of users active in each cluster was identified and the overlap was quantified via Jaccard similarity, defined as the size of the intersection divided by the size of the union (bottom panel of Figure \ref{fig:echo_chambers}). On Bluesky, cross-cluster mention flow was measured to assess whether a post in one space points to an account from the other (top panel of Figure \ref{fig:echo_chambers}).

\subsection{Textual analysis}

For Bluesky, textual analysis was conducted on the cleaned text. A repeated-character normalisation step and SymSpell spell correction were applied to reduce surface variants from informal writing. A protected-term list containing candidate names and common internet tokens was excluded from correction. Post-length distributions after cleaning are reported in Table \ref{tab:bsky_stats}, with the vocabulary-reduction ledger in Table \ref{tab:bsky_prep}. A TF-IDF document-term matrix was then constructed with a minimum-two-character token pattern and no feature cap. The effect of spell correction on the DTM is reported in Table \ref{tab:bsky_dtm_compare}, and the leading distinctive TF-IDF terms per buzz cluster are plotted in Figure \ref{fig:bsky_tfidf_buzzgroups}. Post-type balance and top terms per type are reported in Table \ref{tab:bsky_pt}, addressing whether replies carry comparable political signal to original posts.

Latent Semantic Analysis was conducted in two passes. An initial 100-component TruncatedSVD was fitted to the Bluesky TF-IDF matrix for scree exploration (Figure \ref{fig:bsky_svd_variance}, Table \ref{tab:bsky_thr}); a second TruncatedSVD was then fitted at the 80\%-variance threshold identified from the scree (Table \ref{tab:bsky_lsa_summary}). Top loading terms per latent concept are listed in Table \ref{tab:bsky_concepts}, and weekly trajectories of the leading concepts across the time window are shown in Figure \ref{fig:bsky_lsa_time}.

Fifteen election-relevant terms were tracked as weekly shares per buzz cluster, normalised by within-cluster post volume (Figure \ref{fig:bsky_keyterm_frequency}). TF-IDF-weighted word clouds per buzz cluster are shown in Figure \ref{fig:bsky_wordclouds}.

The same pipeline was applied analogously to Reddit and to newspapers, with Snowball stemming added before TF-IDF to further reduce vocabulary.

\subsection{NLP}

For Bluesky, LDA was applied to the cleaned text which was further preprocessed following the steps in Table \ref{fig:bsky_nlp_preprocessing}. A Gensim dictionary was constructed with no\_below=5, no\_above=0.5 filtering. The number of topics was selected by sweeping K from 2 to 10 with u\_mass coherence and AIC as criteria; the final model was fitted at K=4 (the coherence-optimal value) with asymmetric priors learned from data, 15 passes, and 400 iterations; final LDA model configuration can be seen in Table \ref{tab:bsky_nlp_model_summary} and example terms can be seen in Table \ref{tab:bsky_nlp_topic_words}. Dominant topic assignments per post were then used to produce a topic-over-time series and a topic-share breakdown per buzz cluster.

The same pipeline was applied analogously to Reddit and newspapers, in both cases with candidate names removed from the vocabulary (unlike the Bluesky model, which retained them). Named Entity Recognition was additionally run on all newspaper headlines, which was not done for Bluesky or Reddit. Mention counts over time for the key political figures extracted are plotted in Figure \ref{fig:news_ner_mentions}.

\subsection{Sentiment analysis}

Sentiment analysis was conducted on all three text sources using three lexicon-based methods: VADER, TextBlob, and NRCLex. Applying multiple methods in parallel captures partially distinct aspects of the text. On Bluesky, a fine-tuned transformer, Twitter RoBERTa, was additionally applied to complement the lexicon-based methods with a context-aware, model-based signal specifically trained on social media text; computational limitations prevented extending this to the larger Reddit and newspaper corpora.

The methods were applied to different text inputs per source: for Reddit, to the cleaned text of posts and comments combined; for Bluesky, to post text; and for newspapers, to article headlines.

To bring all methods onto a common scale, several scoring choices were made. VADER compound scores were binned into Positive, Neutral, and Negative using the standard ±0.05 thresholds. TextBlob polarity scores, which already fall in $[-1, +1]$, were used directly with the same ±0.05 thresholds applied for classification. For NRCLex, a scalar sentiment score was derived as the difference between positive and negative word frequencies, yielding a per-document value analogous to the VADER compound. For RoBERTa, a single scalar sentiment score was computed per document as $p(\text{positive}) - p(\text{negative})$, yielding a value in $[-1, +1]$ comparable to the VADER compound.

For each source, mean NRCLex emotion profiles were computed per buzz cluster to compare the eight-emotion breakdown across content spaces (Figures \ref{fig:bluesky_nrc}, \ref{fig:news_nrc}). For newspapers, daily headline sentiment was additionally aggregated per media-leaning group and plotted over the campaign period with key event markers overlaid (Figure \ref{fig:news_vader}), and the three lexicon methods were compared directly on the headline corpus, alongside TextBlob polarity and subjectivity scores per leaning (Figure \ref{fig:news_sentiment_methods}). The distributional properties of the sentiment scores were examined via KDE plots per method and leaning, with a secondary projection by candidate focus (TrumpBuzz vs.\ HarrisBuzz) shown alongside (Figure \ref{fig:news_sentiment_distributions})
